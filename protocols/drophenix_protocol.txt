DroPhenix Analytics — Data Collection & CSV Upload Protocol

PART 1 — CSV TEMPLATE SPECIFICATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Required columns (header row must match exactly):

  Column           Type      Description
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Genotype         text      Fly line name, e.g. Oregon-R_WT
  Treatment        text      Drug / condition, e.g. Spermine_1mM
  Sex              text      Male  OR  Female  (case-sensitive)
  Replicate        integer   Trial number, e.g. 1, 2, 3
  Time             integer   Seconds after tap, e.g. 10, 20, 30, 60
  Count_climbed    integer   Flies ABOVE threshold line
  n_total_flies    integer   All flies in vial (climbed + not climbed)

Minimum experiment:
  ≥ 2 genotypes (at least one must be Wild-type)
  ≥ 2 treatments (at least one must be Vehicle/Untreated)
  Both sexes (required for SDQ calculation)
  ≥ 3 replicates per group
  ≥ 3 time-points (e.g. 10 s, 30 s, 60 s)

Example row:
  Oregon-R_WT, Untreated, Male, 1, 30, 17, 22

Notes:
  - Separator: comma (CSV)
  - No spaces around commas
  - No merged cells (this is NOT Excel formatting)
  - Genotype and Treatment names must be consistent across all rows
    (spelling must match exactly — "Oregon-R_WT" ≠ "OregonWT")
  - Wild-type label and vehicle label are selected in DroPhenix
    during configuration (Data Input → Group Metadata tab)

PART 2 — VIAL PREPARATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Materials:
  • Clear borosilicate glass vials: 25 mm diameter × 95 mm height
    (standard Drosophila culture vials work perfectly)
  • Permanent marker (fine-tip, black or blue)
  • Ruler or printed threshold-line sticker
  • Foam-padded surface for tapping

Threshold line:
  • Mark a horizontal line at 8 cm from the BOTTOM of the vial
  • Flies above this line = "climbed" = Count_climbed
  • Flies at or below this line = "not climbed"
  • Use the same line height for ALL vials in the experiment

Fly density:
  • 15–25 flies per vial (20 is optimal)
  • Do not mix sexes within a vial
  • Use flies aged 3–7 days post-eclosion for best sensitivity
  • Age-match all groups within ± 1 day

Vial labelling:
  • Label each vial on the side (not the bottom) before flies are added
  • Format: Genotype_Treatment_Sex_Replicate
    Example: TDP43_Spermine_Male_Rep1
  • Arrange vials in a consistent grid rack:
      Row    = Genotype (e.g. Row 1 = WT, Row 2 = TDP43, Row 3 = FUS)
      Column = Treatment-Replicate (e.g. Col 1 = Unt_R1, Col 2 = Unt_R2 …)
    Keep Male and Female vials in separate rack sections (left / right)

PART 3 — RECORDING PROTOCOL (Negative Geotaxis)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1 — Acclimatise (30 min before assay)
  Transfer flies to fresh vials (avoids food contamination artefact).
  Optional: fast for 4 h to reduce inter-fly variance.

Step 2 — Camera setup
  Place camera or phone on a tripod at a fixed distance.
  The threshold line on ALL vials must be visible in the same frame.
  Recommended: record all vials simultaneously as a single video.
  Ensure uniform lighting (diffuse, no shadows on threshold line).

Step 3 — Settle period (60 seconds, no tapping)
  Place vials upright in rack. Wait 60 s. Do NOT disturb.
  This allows flies to walk to random positions before the tap.

Step 4 — Tap down and start timer
  Tap vials sharply on the padded surface THREE times in rapid succession.
  Start stopwatch/video timer IMMEDIATELY after the third tap.
  All flies should now be at the bottom of the vial.

Step 5 — Count at each time-point
  Count flies ABOVE the threshold line at:
    T = 10 s   (early climbing burst — sensitive to acute motor function)
    T = 20 s   (intermediate — most discriminative for PI)
    T = 30 s   (plateau start — used for AUC and t50 calculation)
    T = 60 s   (late — detects sustained vs. transient rescue)
  If using video: pause frame at each time-point and count manually.
  If counting live: have one observer per vial OR one observer per row.

Step 6 — Rest interval
  Wait 5 minutes between consecutive trials on the SAME vials.
  This avoids fatigue artefact.
  Each trial = one Replicate in DroPhenix.
  Perform ≥ 3 replicates (= 3 full tap-count cycles per vial).

Step 7 — Enter data
  Open drophenix_data_template.csv (supplied with DroPhenix).
  Enter one row per Vial × Time-point × Replicate.
  Save as CSV (not Excel .xlsx — convert before upload if needed).
  Upload via DroPhenix → Data Input → Upload CSV or Excel.

PART 4 — DRUG TREATMENT GUIDELINES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  Administration route: mix compound in fly food (most common)
    or dissolve in 1% sucrose solution (for compounds insoluble in agar).
  Duration: chronic exposure 7–14 days is recommended for neuromodulators.
  Vehicle control: 1% DMSO in food (if compound is DMSO-soluble)
    OR standard fly food (for water-soluble compounds).
  Dose-response: test ≥ 3 concentrations if doing TCS ranking.
  Label the Treatment column with the compound name AND concentration:
    e.g.  Spermine_1mM   Pantothenate_50uM   Vehicle_1pctDMSO

PART 5 — COMMON ERRORS TO AVOID
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  ✗  Inconsistent genotype names across rows (caps / spelling)
  ✗  Count_climbed > n_total_flies (impossible count — DroPhenix flags this)
  ✗  Missing Sex column or using M/F instead of Male/Female
  ✗  Only one sex group (SDQ cannot be computed)
  ✗  Only one time-point per vial (t50 and AUC require ≥ 3 time-points)
  ✗  Counting within 5 s of the tap (flies still mid-flight — wait 10 s)
  ✗  Different threshold line heights across vials
